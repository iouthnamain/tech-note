# Leadership And Management

This page is for early leadership habits. At this stage, coding quality and execution discipline matter more than formal management techniques.

## 1. Self-Management

Your first leadership job is managing yourself well:

- show up consistently
- finish what you start
- track tasks and blockers
- estimate honestly
- review your week without excuses

## 2. Task Planning And Estimation

For small work items:

- define the goal in one sentence
- list constraints and assumptions
- split work into small visible steps
- estimate with a reason, not a guess
- report early when the estimate is wrong

## 3. Communication And Handoff

Strong communication reduces confusion more than heroics do.

Practice:

- clear progress updates
- blocker reporting
- short decision notes
- clean project handoff summaries

## 4. Code Review And Quality Bar

Leadership shows up in review quality:

- catch bugs early
- explain risk clearly
- keep standards consistent
- separate correctness from preference
- improve shared understanding

- [Group 08: Code Review](../../Full-Stack-Guides/Group-08-Code-Review/README.md)

## 5. Mentoring And Knowledge Sharing

Start small:

- explain one concept clearly
- write short notes after solving a bug
- help someone avoid a mistake you already made
- document patterns that save time

Mentoring at this stage is teaching what you truly understand, not pretending to know everything.

## 6. Small-Scope Coordination

After you ship at least two projects, practice:

- owning a small feature from requirement to handoff
- aligning frontend and backend tasks
- clarifying acceptance criteria
- tracking risks and open questions

## What Not To Optimize For Yet

- people management theory without delivery experience
- status without ownership
- architecture debates without shipped software
- trying to lead before you can execute reliably
