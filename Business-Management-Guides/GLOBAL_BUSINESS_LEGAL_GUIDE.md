## Cultural Dimensions

### Overview

Cultural dimensions help understand cultural differences.

### Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions

#### 1. Power Distance
- Acceptance of inequality
- High: Hierarchical, respect authority
- Low: Egalitarian, question authority

**Examples**:
- High: Malaysia, Philippines
- Low: Denmark, Austria

#### 2. Individualism vs Collectivism
- Individual vs group focus
- Individualistic: Self-reliance, personal achievement
- Collectivistic: Group harmony, group goals

**Examples**:
- Individualistic: USA, Australia
- Collectivistic: Japan, South Korea

#### 3. Masculinity vs Femininity
- Achievement vs relationships
- Masculine: Competition, success, assertiveness
- Feminine: Cooperation, quality of life, modesty

**Examples**:
- Masculine: Japan, Germany
- Feminine: Sweden, Norway

#### 4. Uncertainty Avoidance
- Tolerance for ambiguity
- High: Structure, rules, security
- Low: Flexibility, risk-taking, ambiguity

**Examples**:
- High: Greece, Portugal
- Low: Singapore, Jamaica

#### 5. Long-term vs Short-term Orientation
- Time perspective
- Long-term: Persistence, thrift, future
- Short-term: Tradition, respect, present

**Examples**:
- Long-term: China, Japan
- Short-term: USA, UK

#### 6. Indulgence vs Restraint
- Gratification control
- Indulgent: Enjoy life, leisure, fun
- Restrained: Suppress gratification, strict norms

**Examples**:
- Indulgent: Mexico, Venezuela
- Restrained: Egypt, Pakistan

### Trompenaars' Cultural Dimensions

#### 1. Universalism vs Particularism
- Rules vs relationships
- Universalism: Rules apply to all
- Particularism: Relationships matter more

#### 2. Individualism vs Communitarianism
- Individual vs group
- Similar to Hofstede

#### 3. Specific vs Diffuse
- Separating vs integrating life
- Specific: Separate work and personal
- Diffuse: Integrated life

#### 4. Neutral vs Emotional
- Expression of emotions
- Neutral: Controlled emotions
- Emotional: Open expression

#### 5. Achievement vs Ascription
- Status basis
- Achievement: Based on performance
- Ascription: Based on who you are

#### 6. Sequential vs Synchronic
- Time orientation
- Sequential: One thing at a time
- Synchronic: Multiple things simultaneously

#### 7. Internal vs External Control
- Control orientation
- Internal: Control environment
- External: Adapt to environment
